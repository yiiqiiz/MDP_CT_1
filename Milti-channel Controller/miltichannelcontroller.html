Pressure-Sensitive Interaction Device

This device utilizes a Round Force-Sensitive Resistor (FSR) as its core component to capture variations in finger pressure. By detecting the intensity of the applied pressure, the FSR transmits signals to a Processing program, enabling real-time visual feedback.

In the Processing interface, a dynamic ball adjusts its size according to the pressure level, growing larger as the pressure increases. Simultaneously, the ball's color changes in response to the pressure, offering an intuitive and engaging interactive experience.



Force-Sensitive Resistor (FSR)

FSRs are sensors that allow you to detect physical pressure, squeezing and weight.





Testing

This device demonstrates a simple pressure-sensitive test by connecting a round Force-Sensitive Resistor (FSR) to a light bulb. The FSR changes its resistance based on the pressure applied by a finger.





Final Outcome

It connects a Force-Sensitive Resistor (FSR) to Processing, enabling a real-time interactive visualization. When the FSR is pressed, the system detects the pressure value and sends it to Processing, where it dynamically controls the size and color of a ball displayed on the screen. As the pressure increases, the ball grows larger, and its color shifts based on the intensity of the pressure.
