Liminal Space refers to the strange, transitional realm between two states—whether physical, emotional, or existential. These spaces, like empty hallways, abandoned malls, or foggy highways at dawn, feel both familiar and unsettling, evoking nostalgia, disorientation, or unease. Beyond physical places, liminality also describes life’s in-between moments, such as adolescence, career shifts, or cultural transitions, where one is neither who they were nor who they will become. In art, media, and internet culture, liminal spaces are often depicted as dreamlike voids, resonating with our collective sense of uncertainty and transformation.

Dream Space: Memories of the Laboratory

For this project, I will be creating a liminal dream space inspired by a childhood memory—an old university laboratory building I used to visit frequently. While this space is not a site of fear or trauma, it appears in my dreams as an uncanny, endless structure where I can never find the exit. The dream’s atmosphere is defined by dim, green-tinted lighting, long corridors that seem to stretch indefinitely, and staircases at the end of each hallway leading both up and down, creating a looping, recursive environment.

Sketching the Environment

The space consists of narrow, repetitive hallways lined with tightly packed rooms, each labeled as laboratories. Some rooms contain specimens and scientific artifacts, reinforcing the feeling of an academic yet slightly surreal environment. The combination of repeating objects, cyclical architecture, and non-linear navigation generates an immersive liminal space, emphasizing disorientation and transition rather than resolution.

Interactive Possibilities

Nonlinear Exploration: The experience is not goal-oriented; instead, players will wander freely, retracing their steps only to find that spaces have subtly shifted.
