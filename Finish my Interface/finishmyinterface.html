Bridge of Hands

Bridge of Hands is an interactive art project that combines hand gesture recognition and physics simulation. Using a webcam, the program captures hand movements to create dynamic colored balls and a virtual bridge. When the right hand is detected, colorful balls drop from the tip of the index finger, reflecting playful and creative interactions. The left hand controls the endpoints of a bridge, allowing users to dynamically shape and manipulate its structure through gestures.

Design Process







Step 1: Initialize Libraries and Setup

Loaded ml5.js for hand recognition and Matter.js for physics. Created a canvas and video feed, mirroring the video for intuitive interaction.





Step 2: Hand Gesture Detection

Used ml5.js handPose to track hand positions in real time. Stored keypoints (like fingertips) for both hands to enable interaction.





Step 3: Generate Balls with Right Hand

Detected the right-hand index fingertip to drop colorful, bouncing balls every 30 frames. Added physics properties (bounce, friction) for realistic behavior.





Step 4: Manipulate Bridge with Left Hand

Used the left hand to control the endpoints of a virtual bridge by tracking two fingertips. Updated the bridge's position dynamically based on hand movements.





Step 5: Render and Update

Displayed balls and the bridge on the canvas. Removed balls when they left the screen and updated the bridge with each frame for smooth interaction.
